4x4 RC trucks are an excellent hobby to get involved in.

My Traxxas Slash 4x4

Pictured above is one iteration of my Slash 4x4 (stylized 'Sl4sh') .

I entered the hobby in 2020 after years of wanting to buy a hobby-grade RC truck. I enjoyed watching trophy truck and rally racing, so I picked a 4x4 trophy truck-style platform. The Traxxas Sl4sh fit the bill. Based on great reviews and wide variety of parts, I knew I'd be able to keep upgrading over time to keep the hobby fresh. I had toy-grade RCs growing up, but had very little experience tuning and fixing broken RCs. After breaking a stock A-arm, I dipped my toes into the waters of fix/replace/upgrade maintenance. I upgraded to the Heavy-Duty A-arms.
Slowly, I added upgrades like:

  • Castle 1415 2400kV brushless paired with Mamba X ESC ----- Castle Creations
  • Tekno Big Bone center driveshaft and outdrives ----- Tekno RC
  • Traxxas GTR shocks- GTR Shocks for front -------- GTR Shocks for rear
  • Traxxas Heavy-Duty Constant Velocity Driveshafts ----- Front ----- Back
  • Homemade serial XT60 adapter for 4S power
  • Zeee 2S LiPO batteries (surprisingly high quality and low cost!) ----- Amazon.com
  • Aftermarket body shell from Amazon Summitlink USA Flag Shell

Later, I added Proline Extended Body Mounts to accommodate 2.8" monster truck tires: Pro-Line Sand Paw tires for snow and sand bashing, Pro-Line Trenchers for beast-mode!
